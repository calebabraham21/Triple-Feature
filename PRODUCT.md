# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Friends and people Caleb shares the link with. They're in a specific moment: it's Friday night, someone's already said "let's watch something," and the group has been scrolling Netflix for 20 minutes without committing. They open Triple Feature to end the decision spiral. They're not film critics, not logging their watches, not looking for scores — they want someone with taste to just tell them what to put on.

## Product Purpose

Triple Feature is a movie recommendation engine that gives you three films based on your preferences (genre, decade, runtime, language) instead of infinite scroll. The product is the reduction: three good answers instead of forty mediocre ones. It exists to feel like asking a friend with genuine taste rather than querying a content algorithm. Success means the user picks something within two minutes and feels good about it.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, Opinionated, Intimate.

Triple Feature has a point of view. It recommends; it doesn't aggregate. It feels handcrafted — like a zine or a curated playlist, not a platform. The creator's presence is appropriate and intentional: this is Caleb's thing, and that personal investment is what gives the recommendations credibility. Warm enough to be inviting, dark enough to take film seriously.

## Anti-references

- **Letterboxd**: social film logging, community feeds, diary format, critic culture. Triple Feature is not a logging tool and has no community layer.
- **Netflix / streaming services**: infinite scroll, algorithm-first, corporate content grids, genre carousels. The whole product is a reaction against this.
- **Generic SaaS dark mode**: the dark-blue sidebar + card grid template. Dark for dark's sake, no personality, interchangeable with any other B2B tool.

## Design Principles

1. **The answer in three, not thirty.** Every screen gives you less, not more. The reduction is the product. Resist the urge to surface more options, more data, more controls.
2. **Taste over algorithm.** The interface should feel like it has preferences, not like a neutral query engine. Choices in typography, color, and copy should feel deliberate and personal.
3. **The creator is in the room.** This is Caleb's project. The personal voice belongs on every surface — in the copy, the about pages, the editorial choices. Anonymizing it would be a mistake.
4. **Dark because the content is dark.** The cinema aesthetic earns its darkness from the subject matter — projection rooms, film grain, late-night screens. Not because dark is a trend.
5. **Conviction over consensus.** Triple Feature tells you what to watch. It doesn't hedge with aggregated scores, critic percentages, or "because others watched." The interface should project the same confidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios. Full keyboard navigation. No additional accommodation requirements documented at this time.
